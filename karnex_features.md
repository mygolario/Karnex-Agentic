# Karnex — Full Feature Blueprint
### The AI Operating System for Solo Founders

---

## The Core Philosophy

> Karnex is not a chatbot. It is a **co-founder that executes**.
> It starts from your raw ambition — not a polished idea — and runs the actual work of building a startup alongside you.

Your target user is someone exactly like you: big dreams, zero co-founder, no huge team, limited time, high ambition. They don't need more information. They need **execution**.

---

## 🧠 Layer 1 — The Dream Engine (Idea Intelligence)

This is what makes Karnex unique from every other agentic tool. It starts *before* the code.

### 1.1 Pain-to-Product Transformer
- User dumps their raw frustrations, daily annoyances, or life experiences in any format (voice, text, document)
- An agent analyzes the dump and extracts latent pains, patterns, and recurring themes
- Maps each pain to potential product archetypes (SaaS, marketplace, API, community, tool)
- Outputs a ranked list of 5–10 real product opportunities grounded in the user's lived experience
- **"Your pain is your moat"** — the system always starts from what the founder *personally understands*

### 1.2 Idea Crystallizer
- Takes a vague idea ("I want to build something for restaurants") and runs a structured interrogation
- Asks sharp, Socratic questions across 6 dimensions: Who suffers? How much do they suffer? What's the workaround today? What's the unlock? What's the business model? What's defensible?
- Synthesizes answers into a tight 1-page idea memo in real time
- Scores the idea across: Market size, Founder-market fit, Speed to MVP, Competition density, Monetization clarity

### 1.3 Trend Radar Agent
- Continuously scans YC batch releases, Product Hunt, Indie Hackers, Twitter/X, Reddit, and VC thesis posts
- Surfaces white spaces — areas with growing pain but no dominant solution yet
- Weekly brief: "3 opportunities Karnex spotted this week that match your profile"
- Lets the founder filter by: B2B vs B2C, technical vs no-code, bootstrappable vs VC-backable

### 1.4 Competitive Landscape Agent
- Given an idea, auto-builds a real competitor map (pulls live data from Crunchbase, ProductHunt, G2, App Store)
- Finds the "gap table" — what every competitor is missing
- Identifies the positioning wedge: what exact angle does no one own yet
- Outputs a one-pager: "Here is the white space. Here is your entry point."

### 1.5 ICP (Ideal Customer Profile) Definer
- Asks the founder about their network, domain expertise, and past experience
- Cross-references with the idea to define the single sharpest ICP
- Generates 3 detailed customer personas with: job title, daily frustrations, current tools, budget authority, watering holes (where they hang out online)
- Persona profiles are used by ALL downstream execution agents

## 🗺️ Layer 2 — The Architect (Roadmap & Strategy)

### 2.1 The 90-Day War Room
- After idea crystallization, Karnex generates a full 90-day founder roadmap
- Broken into 3 phases: Validation (days 1–30), MVP (days 31–60), First Revenue (days 61–90)
- Each day has: 1 primary goal, 3 tasks, a success metric, and a "skip if" condition (to avoid perfectionism)
- Roadmap adapts in real time based on what's done, delayed, or pivoted

### 2.2 Sprint Planner Agent
- Converts the roadmap into weekly sprints with daily task cards
- Each task card contains: what to do, why it matters, how long it should take, and which Karnex agent can execute it for you
- Drag-and-drop prioritization
- Automatic re-planning when you fall behind or change direction

### 2.3 Decision Journal
- Every major founder decision (pivot, pricing, tech stack, target market) is logged
- Each decision entry includes: context at the time, options considered, choice made, expected outcome
- 30 days later, Karnex checks back: "You decided X. Here's what happened. Would you change it?"
- Builds a personal founder wisdom layer over time

### 2.4 Milestone Tracker
- Visual milestone map from "idea" to "product-market fit"
- Each milestone has: definition, success criteria, and the 3 blocking tasks to clear it
- Unlocks new agents and capabilities as milestones are hit (gamified progression)

### 2.5 Risk Radar
- Scans the current plan and flags the top 3 existential risks every week
- Each risk comes with: likelihood, severity, and a mitigation playbook
- Examples: "Your MVP timeline assumes you'll get 10 beta users — here's a backup plan if you only get 2"

## ⚙️ Layer 3 — The Executor Pack (Agents That Actually Do Things)

This is the core engine. Agents that don't advise — they ship.

### 3.1 Builder Agent (Code & Product)
- Generates full MVP codebases from a product spec
- Scaffolds: landing page, auth, payments (Stripe), database schema, API, admin dashboard
- Iterates based on feedback in plain English: "Make the onboarding flow 2 steps shorter"
- Connects to GitHub, Vercel, Railway — deploys automatically
- Can generate and push code commits without the founder writing a line
- Supports: Next.js, React, Python/FastAPI, Supabase — opinionated stack for solo founders

### 3.2 Research Agent
- Deep-dives any topic: market size, regulatory landscape, pricing benchmarks, technology options
- Returns structured research briefs — not a wall of text, but a decision-ready document
- Can read and summarize competitor documentation, pricing pages, App Store reviews, G2 reviews
- Outputs: "Here is what I found. Here is what it means for your decision."

### 3.3 Outreach Agent
- Writes personalized cold emails and LinkedIn messages for: beta user recruitment, investor outreach, partnership exploration
- Learns the founder's voice from past writing samples
- Manages outreach sequences: follow-up 1 after 3 days, follow-up 2 after 7 days, close loop at day 14
- Tracks open rates and reply rates, rewrites subject lines that underperform
- Integrates with Gmail and LinkedIn (via API or browser extension)

### 3.4 Content & SEO Agent
- Writes blog posts, Twitter threads, LinkedIn essays, and case studies
- Generates a 3-month content calendar aligned to the ICP's search intent
- Does keyword research and builds programmatic SEO content at scale
- Turns user interviews and customer wins into case study drafts
- Auto-publishes to Ghost, Webflow, or the founder's own blog

### 3.5 Sales Agent
- Writes and runs cold email campaigns for the first 10 paying customers
- Prepares a discovery call script tailored to the ICP
- After calls, auto-generates: follow-up email, proposal draft, objection responses
- Creates a basic CRM view: lead status, last touchpoint, next action
- Integrates with Notion, Airtable, or its own lightweight CRM

### 3.6 Design Agent
- Generates: landing page copy + layout brief, onboarding flow wireframes, email templates
- Creates brand identity starters: name options, color palette, typography pairings, logo concepts (via image gen)
- Writes Tailwind/CSS component specs that the Builder Agent can implement directly
- Can critique existing designs: "Here are 5 things killing your conversion rate"

### 3.7 Financial Modeling Agent
- Builds a 12-month P&L model from: pricing, assumed conversion rates, CAC estimate, churn assumption
- Calculates: runway, break-even, LTV:CAC ratio
- Runs "what if" scenarios: "What if I raise pricing by 30%? What if churn doubles?"
- Generates investor-ready financial slides from the model
- Tracks actual vs modeled metrics once revenue starts

### 3.8 Legal & Compliance Agent
- Generates first-draft documents: Terms of Service, Privacy Policy, SaaS Agreement, NDA
- Answers common founder legal questions: GDPR basics, Delaware C-corp vs LLC, IP assignment
- Flags regulatory risks for specific industries (fintech, health, edtech)
- Always disclaims: "Not legal advice. Review with a lawyer before signing."

### 3.9 Fundraising Agent
- Writes and iterates on: pitch deck narrative, executive summary, investor email
- Builds a target investor list based on: stage, check size, thesis alignment, portfolio overlap
- Runs personalized outreach sequences to 50–100 angels/seed funds
- Tracks: sent, opened, replied, meeting booked, passed
- Prepares Q&A prep doc for every investor meeting (pulls from their public interviews and portfolio)

### 3.10 Analytics & Insight Agent
- Connects to: Stripe, PostHog, Mixpanel, Google Analytics, Intercom
- Weekly digest: "Here is what the data says. Here is what you should do about it."
- Flags anomalies: sudden churn spike, traffic drop, activation rate dip
- Suggests A/B tests based on funnel drop-off points
- Summarizes user feedback from support tickets, reviews, and NPS responses

## 🧭 Layer 4 — The Compass (Coaching, Accountability & Motivation)

This layer is what makes Karnex different from every executor-only tool. It's the mentor the founder never had.

### 4.1 The Daily Standup
- Every morning: Karnex sends a 3-question check-in (90 seconds to complete)
  - "What's your one must-win today?"
  - "What's blocking you right now?"
  - "What did yesterday teach you?"
- Responses feed into the sprint tracker and flag blockers to the coaching layer

### 4.2 The Weekly Debrief
- Every Friday: Karnex generates a weekly founder report
  - What was planned vs what was done
  - Key learnings from the week
  - Top 3 priorities for next week
  - One hard question the founder needs to answer before Monday
- Pushes the debrief to email or Notion

### 4.3 The Momentum Score
- A single 0–100 score updated daily
- Composed of: task completion rate, streak consistency, milestone proximity, user/revenue growth
- When Momentum drops below 40 for 3 days: Karnex triggers an intervention
  - Not a generic motivational quote
  - A specific, structured "get unstuck" session: "Let's figure out why this is stuck and what we do today."

### 4.4 Accountability Mode
- Founder declares a public commitment: "I will launch by [date]"
- Karnex holds them to it — daily check-ins escalate as the date approaches
- Optional: share the commitment with a trusted contact who gets weekly updates
- Post-deadline: structured debrief, no judgment, recalibrate

### 4.5 The Mirror Agent (Anti-Delusion Layer)
- Proactively challenges founder assumptions:
  - "You've been building for 6 weeks but haven't talked to a customer. Let's fix that today."
  - "Your roadmap has 47 tasks this month. A solo founder can do ~12. Which 12 actually matter?"
  - "You've pivoted 3 times in 8 weeks. Let's talk about whether this is iteration or avoidance."
- Not brutal — honest and specific, like a good mentor
- Can be toggled: Gentle / Direct / Brutal (the founder chooses their mode)

### 4.6 The Founder Knowledge Graph
- Karnex builds a private model of the founder over time:
  - Strengths, blind spots, recurring procrastination patterns
  - Tasks they always delay (usually sales-related)
  - Time of day they're most productive
  - Emotional signals from daily standups
- Used to personalize coaching, task scheduling, and agent recommendations
- Always private, never shared, deletable at any time

### 4.7 The Mentor Library
- Curated insight library from top founders, investors, and operators
- Not generic advice — filtered by: founder's current stage, industry, challenge type
- "You're struggling with pricing. Here's how 5 founders who solved this thought about it."
- Surfaces relevant content exactly when it's needed, not as a content dump

## 🏗️ Layer 5 — Platform & Infrastructure

### 5.1 The Mission Control Dashboard
- Single-pane view of: active tasks, agent status, momentum score, key metrics
- Drag agents onto a task like tools on a workbench
- Real-time feed of what every agent is doing right now ("Outreach Agent sent 12 emails. 3 opened.")

### 5.2 Karnex Memory
- Persistent memory across all sessions
- Remembers: the startup idea, ICPs, past decisions, tone of voice, preferred tools
- Every agent is context-aware — the Sales Agent knows what the Builder Agent shipped last week

### 5.3 Agent Orchestration
- Founder can chain agents: "Research competitors → write a positioning document → build a landing page based on it"
- Agents hand off context to each other automatically
- One instruction triggers a multi-agent workflow

### 5.4 Integrations Hub
- GitHub, Vercel, Railway (deploy)
- Stripe (revenue tracking)
- Gmail, LinkedIn (outreach)
- Notion, Airtable (ops)
- PostHog, Mixpanel, GA4 (analytics)
- Intercom, Crisp (support)
- Cal.com (scheduling)
- Slack (notifications)

### 5.5 The Founder Vault
- Secure private storage for: API keys, financial models, pitch decks, customer contracts
- All agent outputs auto-saved and versioned
- One-click export of everything you've built in Karnex
